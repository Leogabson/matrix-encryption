Hill Cipher

Matrix-based polygraphic substitution over A–Z (mod 26).

Note: The Hill cipher is a classical cipher and is cryptographically weak — it is vulnerable to known-plaintext attack with as few as n² letter pairs. Use AES-GCM for any security-sensitive application.

Must be square and invertible mod 26. Numbers must align — use monospace to verify columns.
